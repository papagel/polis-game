System guide & engineering overview

POLIS

An isometric city-builder that lives in a single HTML file — no build, no dependencies, no framework. This deck is two things at once: a how-to-play for new mayors and an engineering tour of the machine underneath.

⚙ Vanilla JS + Canvas 2D 📄 One file · ~12,950 lines 🚀 Zero dependencies 🌐 polis-game.com
The premise

One file. A whole city.

Everything — the renderer, the economy, the music, the save system — is global code inside one index.html. That constraint is a feature: the game runs by simply opening the file, and it ships in seconds.

🧩

Self-contained

No bundler, no npm, no JSX. Only external resource is a Google Fonts <link>.

🎨

Procedural art

Every building is drawn from geometry on a Canvas — boxes, volumes, windows, gables. No sprites.

📱

Mobile-first

Pointer events, pinch-zoom, DPR-aware canvas, installable as a PWA with a drawn icon.

🔊

Generative audio

WebAudio composes the soundtrack and SFX live — nothing is pre-recorded.

Design rule — the single-file, zero-dependency nature is core. No build step is ever introduced; changes are live on reload.

Inside index.html

899–12948<script> — the entire game engine

Because everything is global, you navigate by symbol name, not modules. Three entry points hold it together:

  • loop() — the requestAnimationFrame frame loop
  • simTick() — advances one game day
  • render() — draws one frame (painter's algorithm)
Decoupled — rendering is throttled by a frame governor, but the simulation always runs at full fidelity regardless of FPS.

Engineering · the heart of the game

How a lot grows

A zoned lot levels up only when every gate is satisfied. Miss one and it stalls — miss it for long and it decays back down.

Adjacent road

It needs a road touching it — roads carry cars, power & water.

Power & water

Both must flood-fill to the tile through the network.

Demand + land value

Positive RCI demand, and land value over rising thresholds.

Education + leisure

High tiers also require an educated workforce and nearby amenities.

Towers — a mature level-3 lot calls tryMerge() on its four neighbors to form a 2×2 tower, which can climb to skyscraper, highrise, and megatower. Lose power and unmerge() breaks it back down — or to rubble after a fire.
Levels 1 → 7 2×2 towers CAP = pop/jobs per tier
Engineering · power, water, traffic

The networks under the streets

Roads are the nervous system — they carry cars, power, and water all at once. Two flood-fills and a decaying traffic field decide whether your city thrives or chokes.

⚡ Power

recomputePower() flood-fills conductivity from plants across roads & buildings. Coal → Solar → Nuclear → Fusion, each with a reach and a smog cost.

💧 Water

recomputeWater() floods from pumps (need a riverbank) and towers (anywhere). Same network shape, different source.

🚗 Traffic

A Float32 field decays each day. congestion = traffic / ROAD_CAP; gridlock throttles growth and land value.

Golden rule of editing — after mutating the map, call recomputeNets() + recomputeFields() and set dirty = true, or the change won't show.

How to play · the opening

Your first five minutes

Order matters. A lot can't grow without power and water reaching it down a road — so lay the bones before you zone.

Drop a power plant

Start with a Coal plant or a clean Solar farm. Place it at the map edge — smog drifts.

Add water

A Water tower works anywhere; a Pump needs a riverbank but holds far more.

Lay a road spine

Roads carry the power and water you just made out to the lots.

Zone R, C, I along it

Residential first for people, then Commercial & Industrial for jobs. Watch the RCI bars.

The Advisor (💡) watches over new cities and points out exactly what's missing — power, water, jobs, or a road.
How to play · the toolbox

Everything you can build

Road · Avenue (3× capacity) · Footpath · Bus stop · Elevated rail · Rail station. Rail crosses water & roads on pylons.

Residential R Commercial C Industrial I — paint them and let demand do the rest.

⚡ Utilities

Coal · Solar · Nuclear · Fusion plants; Water pump & tower; Landfill, Recycling, Incinerator for garbage.

🏛 Civic

Police & Fire, Schools → University, Clinics → Medical Center. Each projects a coverage radius.

🌳 Leisure

Parks, plazas, gardens, pools, stadiums, zoo, seaport, airport — all lift nearby land value.

⛰ Terrain

Plant trees, dig lakes, level ground, and raise or lower hills to sculpt the land itself.

Mastery · gameplay tricks

Mayor's cheat sheet

🚌 Beat gridlock

A Bus stop cuts driving 45% for homes within 4 tiles; a Rail station reaches 6. Use Avenues (3× capacity) on main arteries.

🌳 Farm land value

Parks, plazas & gardens raise value over a radius — that's what unlocks higher tiers and dense towers.

🎓 Educate to densify

Top tiers need an educated workforce. A University + the Education Grants ordinance pushes the skyline up.

🌫 Bank the smog

Put coal plants & industry downwind at the edge. Go Solar/Nuclear/Fusion and run Smog Controls as you scale.

🌊 Build for floods

In the Flood Plain, elevated rail doesn't mind water — route transit over the river and rebuild from the banks.

🗑 Don't forget garbage

Sanitation collects trash over the road network. Recycling is clean; landfill is cheap but smelly.

Engineering · save, test, ship

Saving, testing & shipping

💾 Save format

A city encodes to TLV5.<base64(JSON)> — per-cell [type, lv, dev, bitmask] plus state. The version prefix bumps when the payload shape changes; older codes still load.

🧪 Tests

A headless Playwright suite loads the real file and asserts invariants — save round-trips, the budget identity, growth gating, flood-fills, loan amortization — not golden numbers, so honest tuning stays green.

🚀 Deploy

Hosted on GitHub Pages at polis-game.com. Deploy = commit + push; the live site updates within a minute. CNAME pins the domain.

Tile types serialize by array index — appended to TYPE_IDS, never reordered, or every saved city breaks. Order is load-bearing.
